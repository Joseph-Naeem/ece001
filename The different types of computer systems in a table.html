The different types of computer systems in a table:

1. Mainframe Computer 2. Desktop Computer 4. Palmtop Computer or Personal Digital Assistant (PDA)
#A mainframe computer is a computer system with:
• very powerful processors
• lots of backing storage
• large internal memory.
 #A desktop computer is the most common kind of PC.
It is a collection of a number of different hardware devices.		 #This type of computer is increasing in popularity, and is often called a Personal Digital Assistant (PDA).
A palmtop computer is small enough to fit in your pocket.
It combines a lot of capabilities, including organiser features (such as storing contact numbers, names and addresses, etc.), e-mail and wireless internet access.
#Mainframes are designed to process large volumes of data at high speed.
They are used by large businesses such as:
• banks and mail-order
• companies as well as by large ; organisations such as universities.
 #This type of computer is sited permanently on a desk because its design means it cannot be easily moved. #Palmtop's have small keyboards and most let you open menus and select icons by using a special pen or stylus.
Most let you enter data by writing with the stylus.
They are powered by batteries and store their data on removable memory units called flash cards.
#Mainframe computers can also multi-task by running more than one program at the same time time.

 #You can run a wide range of software on palmtop's, for example simple word processing, database and spreadsheet software as well as useful applications such as electronic diaries.
